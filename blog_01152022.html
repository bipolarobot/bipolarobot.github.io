BLEND-ing In

Jan 15 - Written by Cal

After the 30+ hours working in Unity (and finishing the Udemy course!), I took a break and decided to dive into Blender due to my curiosity of 3D modeling. The image displayed above is my progress from a YouTube series by CrossMind Studio. So far, I’ve completed their "Blender Day 1 - Absolute Basics - Introduction Series for Beginners" which was a little over an hour long

From just that hour, I learned:

  • Moving around the camera view (at first, this was very difficult to get use to but it gets much easier after practicing the camera movements)
  • Hot-keys for basic/complex scaling and moving
  • Duplication and aligning
  • Global and local axis movement, rotation, and scaling
  • Altering and adding vertices
  • Simple material application to a low-poly design

I found it helpful to make a reference sheet as a form of practice which made it easier to memorize the hot-keys:

But Blender also has a list of their hot-keys in the form of a PDF: https://download.blender.org/documentation/BlenderHotkeyReference.pdf

I then followed up with starting some of my own designs. This included hardwood flooring, a TV, and a box truck. I started out small with low-poly type designs and gradually added more detail once I felt more comfortable. I’ve never been good at drawing perceptions in art but I would say Blender is a whole new ball game which made going into this program a lot less daunting. After getting more acquainted with the hot-keys (and having reference photos on hand), it became easier and easier to move around and create 3D models.
